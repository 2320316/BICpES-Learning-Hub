Overview

What is Tinkercad?

Tinkercad is a free, browser-based design tool from Autodesk that covers 3D modeling, circuit simulation, and codeblock programming. It requires no installation — just a free account and a modern browser. It's particularly powerful for CPE students learning Arduino for the first time.

The Circuits module lets you drag-and-drop real components (resistors, LEDs, sensors, Arduino Uno/Nano, breadboards) and simulate live behavior with voltage probes and a serial monitor — making it an excellent sandbox before building on real hardware.

Access

How to Get Started

1
Open Tinkercad in Your Browser
Navigate to tinkercad.com — no download required. Tinkercad runs entirely in-browser and is compatible with Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari. A stable internet connection is required.
2
Create a Free Autodesk Account
Click Sign Up and register using your school email. Students under 13 require parental/guardian consent. Your instructor may also provide a Classroom link that auto-enrolls you into the course workspace.

💡 Using your school email qualifies you for Autodesk Education benefits including access to additional Autodesk tools.

3
Navigate to Circuits
From the Tinkercad dashboard, click Circuits in the left sidebar. Click Create new Circuit to open the simulation workspace. The interface shows the component panel on the right, canvas in the center, and simulation controls at the top.
4
Build & Simulate
Drag components from the right panel onto the breadboard area. Connect them by clicking wire endpoints. To add Arduino code, click the Arduino component and select Code. Press Start Simulation to run the circuit and observe live behavior.

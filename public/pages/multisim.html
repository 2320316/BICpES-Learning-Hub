Overview

What is Multisim?

NI Multisim is an industry-standard SPICE-based circuit simulation environment designed for education and professional use. It allows you to design, simulate, and analyze electronic circuits without needing physical components — saving time and reducing the risk of damaging hardware during prototyping.

In BICpES, Multisim is used across subjects such as Circuit Analysis, Electronics 1 & 2, and Embedded Systems to verify designs, run virtual experiments, and generate waveform data for reports.

Steps to Download

Installation Guide

1
Visit the NI Website
Go to ni.com/multisim or use the direct download link provided below. Make sure you're connected to a stable internet connection as the installer is approximately 6–8 GB.
2
Create or Log In to an NI Account
Register for a free NI account using your school email address. Students may qualify for free academic licensing through the NI Academic Site License program — check with your instructor for the institution's license key.
3
Download the Installer
Select NI Multisim 14.x (latest stable version) and choose the Full version. Download the .exe installer to your PC. This may take 15–30 minutes depending on your connection speed.
4
Run the Installer
Right-click the installer and select Run as Administrator. Follow the setup wizard — accept the license agreement, select installation directory, and choose NI Circuit Design Suite which includes Multisim and Ultiboard. Click Install and wait for completion.

⚠ Disable antivirus temporarily if the installer is blocked. Windows Defender may flag NI installers; this is a false positive.

5
Activate with License Key
On first launch, Multisim will prompt for activation. Enter the serial number provided by your instructor or obtained through the NI Academic program. Alternatively, select Try for 30 days to begin immediately.
